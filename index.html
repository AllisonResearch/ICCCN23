Workshop Scope

Multi- Agent Systems (MAS) has rapidly emerged as an integral sub-field of Artificial Intelligence and has presented new and exciting challenges to the community of networking and communications researchers. Within a Multi Agent System, several decision making agents interact within a shared environments towards a common, or conflicting goal. Multi- Robot Systems (MRS) are a subset of MAS with physical embodiment that presents a unique set of challenges and applications. Fundamentally, the cooperation required of both MAS and MRS in achieving their objectives places arduous demands of the networking infrastructure, and seeks novel communication solutions.

This workshop aims to congeal about the community interested in MAS and MRS especially as concerned to communication and coordination challenges. It intends to facilitate discussions about contemporary problems and solutions, towards defining standardized scientific methods and practices for the design, development and validation of algorithms and strategies in these domains.

Topics

  • Communication in MAS or MRS
  • Modeling of MAS or MRS
  • Heterogeneity of sensing, motion and computation capabilities in MAS or MRS
  • Bio-Inspired Systems Intelligence
  • Security, Control and Optimization Methods
  • Networking approaches in MAS or MRS
  • Control Architectures, Communication and Scalability
  • Planning and Decision Making
  • MRS Motion Planning and Coordination
  • Task Planning, Allocation, and Cooperative Execution
  • Cooperative Decision Making in MAS or MRS
  • Human interactions with MAS or MRS
  • Cooperative/Collective Learning in MAS or MRS
  • Industrial/Societal Applications
  • Operating Systems and Cloud Technology
  • Software Platforms and Realistic Simulation Tools
  • Principled Experimental Methodologies for MAS or MRS
  • MRS in Unstructured Environments
  • MRS applications in disaster response, search & rescue operations, etc.
  • Performance Evaluation and Benchmarking in MAS or MRS
